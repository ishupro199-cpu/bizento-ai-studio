## PART 2: The 4 Ad Formats — Deep Explanation

### FORMAT 1: Model / Influencer

**What it is:** AI generates a realistic human model naturally using or wearing the product. The model looks like a real influencer or model — not AI-looking, not stock photo-looking. The product is clearly visible.

**Key rules:**
- Model must look like a real person — no uncanny valley, no plastic skin
- Product must be visible, identifiable, correctly proportioned
- Model's interaction with product must look natural (actually holding, wearing, using)
- Background supports the story — not distracting
- Model ethnicity: default Indian (fair to medium-dark skin tones) unless user specifies
- No explicit faces on model unless user requests — safe default is lifestyle angle (profile, 3/4 view, hands visible, face partial)

**Model types available:**
```
Young Woman (18-28)     → Beauty, fashion, lifestyle, skincare
Young Man (22-32)       → Electronics, sports, fashion, grooming
Professional Woman      → Office products, premium bags, business wear
Professional Man        → Watches, suits, premium accessories
Fitness Woman/Man       → Sports gear, supplements, activewear
Homemaker/Mother        → Home products, kids items, kitchen
Hand-only               → Jewelry, watches, cosmetics, food — safest option
Couple                  → Gifting, lifestyle, home products
Friends group           → Youth products, snacks, entertainment
Child (6-12)            → Toys, kids fashion, school supplies
```

---

### FORMAT 2: CGI / Macro Cinematic

**What it is:** Product treated as the main character in a cinematic scene — no human model. Product floats, spins, emerges from liquid/particles/light. Looks like a premium TV commercial for a global brand.

**Key rules:**
- Product proportions must be 100% accurate
- CGI elements (particles, liquid, light beams) must enhance — never obscure the product
- Background is abstract/atmospheric — deep space, dark void, liquid surface, particle field
- Color grade is dramatic and intentional
- This style works even for simple products — a shampoo bottle in a water splash = premium
- Think: Apple iPhone reveal, Chanel No.5 commercial, Red Bull energy ad

**CGI scene types:**
```
Splash / Liquid         → Product emerging from or surrounded by liquid (water, oil, milk, color)
Particle / Dust         → Product surrounded by floating particles, powder, glitter, sand
Light Rays              → Product lit by dramatic god rays, lens flares, prismatic light
Space / Float           → Product floating in deep space or dark void with stars
Fire / Smoke            → Product with atmospheric smoke, steam, or fire elements
Crystal / Glass         → Product on crystal/glass surface, refractions and caustics
Nature Macro            → Product surrounded by extreme close-up nature: petals, leaves, droplets
Motion Blur             → Product shown as if in fast motion, trails, speed lines
```

---

### FORMAT 3: Scene Builder

**What it is:** Product placed in a fully styled, aspirational lifestyle scene — but the scene itself is cinematic, not just a lifestyle photo. Cinematic camera angle, dramatic lighting, story in a single frame.

**Key rules:**
- Scene must look like a movie still or high-end commercial still
- Depth of field: product sharp, background cinematic blur
- Lighting: directional, dramatic — not flat
- Scene fully realized: furniture, environment, context all present
- No model visible (or hands only optional)
- Scene perfectly matches the product's target lifestyle

**Scene presets:**
```
Morning Luxury          → Bedroom, sunrise light, coffee + product on bedside/table
Urban Professional      → Modern office, city view, product on desk
Evening Glow            → Warm candlelit table, product with wine/book
Outdoor Adventure       → Mountain/forest, golden light, product on rock
Beach / Coastal         → Sandy surface, ocean in background, product in scene
Kitchen / Home          → Premium kitchen counter, product with ingredients
Gym / Fitness Studio    → Gym environment, equipment, product on bench
Rooftop Party           → City night lights, bokeh, product in social setting
Luxury Hotel            → Marble surfaces, expensive furniture, product in scene
Minimalist Interior     → Scandinavian/Japanese interior, product as design object
```

---

### FORMAT 4: Brand Story

**What it is:** A single powerful image that tells an emotional story about why the product exists — not what it is, but what it means to the user. Concept-driven, sometimes abstract, always emotionally resonant.

**Key rules:**
- Must have a clear emotional concept (confidence, freedom, care, ambition)
- Product may be smaller in frame — emotion is the hero
- Text overlay zone: always leave 30-40% of frame as clean negative space for headline text
- Color palette must reinforce the emotion
- Think: Dove "Real Beauty", Nike "Just Do It", Tanishq Indian wedding ads

**Emotion-to-visual mapping:**
```
Confidence          → Product with bold lighting, person standing tall (if model), warm gold tones
Freedom             → Open outdoor scene, product in motion/action, bright sky
Care / Love         → Warm soft light, gentle hands, pastel/warm palette, soft bokeh
Ambition            → Dark dramatic background, product lit like a trophy, cool-warm contrast
Freshness / Energy  → Bright clean colors, sharp lighting, dynamic composition
Elegance            → Dark luxury setting, minimal elements, product as jewel
Joy / Celebration   → Bright vibrant colors, movement, confetti, warm golden light
Achievement         → Product as reward, dramatic hero lighting, strong shadow
```

## PART 5: Aspect Ratio Configuration

### Per platform, adjust prompt composition

```
9:16 (Instagram Reels / YouTube Shorts / Stories):
→ "vertical 9:16 composition, product positioned in center-lower third,
   dramatic sky/ceiling/space in upper portion,
   bottom third clean for text overlay (CTA, price, handle)"

1:1 (Instagram Feed / Facebook Post):
→ "square 1:1 composition, product centered, 
   equal breathing space on all sides,
   balanced composition, no important elements cut at edges"

4:5 (Instagram Feed — optimal):
→ "portrait 4:5 composition, product in upper-center,
   lower portion available for text overlay,
   most visual real estate in Instagram feed"

16:9 (YouTube thumbnail / Facebook cover / Website banner):
→ "widescreen 16:9 composition, product as left-side or center hero,
   right side available for text (title, offer, CTA),
   cinematic horizontal feeling, wide establishing shot energy"

3:4 (Pinterest / Print):
→ "tall portrait 3:4 composition, product at visual center,
   top and bottom space for title and details"
```

## PART 10: Quality Control — Cinematic Standard

### Pass/fail checklist before showing to user

```
□ Does this look like a real commercial/advertisement?
□ Is the product clearly identifiable and correctly colored?
□ Does the lighting match the chosen mood?
□ Is the color grade applied correctly (not flat/neutral when dramatic was requested)?
□ For Model format: does the model look like a real person (not AI-obvious)?
□ For Model format: is the product correctly scaled and positioned in the model's hands/body?
□ For CGI format: are the CGI elements (particles/liquid/light) photorealistic?
□ For Scene Builder: does the scene look aspirational and real?
□ Is there clear visual hierarchy — one main subject, supporting elements?
□ Would this image stop someone scrolling on Instagram?
□ Is there proper breathing space / negative space in composition?
```

### Auto-regeneration trigger

If more than 2 checklist items fail → auto-regenerate ONCE before showing.
If second also fails → show with flag and regenerate button.

### Benchmark targets

```
Model/Influencer → Nykaa, Myntra, Mamaearth campaign quality
```
