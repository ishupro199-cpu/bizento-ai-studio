# Pixalera — Ads Creation Tool
## Complete AI Model Training Document + Prompts v1.0

---

## WHAT THIS TOOL DOES (vs other tools)

| | Cinematic Ads | Ads Creation |
|---|---|---|
| Output | 1 cinematic image/frame | Full campaign pack |
| Focus | Visual storytelling | Marketing conversion |
| Contains | Image only | Image + copy + hashtags + CTA |
| Used for | Brand building | Direct sales, promotions, daily posts |
| Thinks like | Film director | Performance marketer |
| Benchmark | Nike, Dior campaign | Mamaearth, boAt, Nykaa Facebook ads |

This tool thinks like a performance marketing expert. It analyzes the product, understands the audience, writes persuasive copy in 3 tones, generates a platform-ready visual creative, and packages everything as a ready-to-post campaign.

## PART 1: Marketing Intelligence Analysis

### What to extract from the uploaded image

This is deeper than just product analysis — it extracts marketing signals.

```
MARKETING ANALYSIS SCHEMA:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
product_name:            [specific]
product_category:        [detailed subcategory]
price_segment:           [Budget under ₹500 / Mid ₹500-2000 / Premium ₹2000-10000 / Luxury 10000+]
                         [Infer from product appearance, packaging, brand if visible]
primary_usp:             [the ONE thing that makes this product special]
                         [e.g.: "ceramic mug → keeps coffee hot 3x longer"]
secondary_usps:          [2-3 additional benefits]
target_audience:         [specific: "working women 25-35 who value aesthetics"]
pain_point_solved:       [what problem does this product solve?]
                         [e.g.: "mug → ugly office cups, disposable cups waste"]
desire_triggered:        [what aspiration does it tap into?]
                         [e.g.: "mug → aesthetic workspace, self-care morning routine"]
seasonal_relevance:      [check if product fits Diwali/Holi/Eid/Valentine/Monsoon etc.]
gifting_potential:       [High / Medium / Low]
impulse_buy_score:       [High / Medium / Low — based on price + desire]
best_hook_style:         [Problem-solution / Aspirational / Social proof / Offer-led / Curiosity]
platform_fit:            [rank Instagram/Facebook/YouTube/WhatsApp 1-4 for this product]
```

### Confidence display to user

After analysis, show marketing brief cards animating in:

```
Card 1: "Product: [name]"
Card 2: "Target audience: [specific audience]"  
Card 3: "Core USP: [USP]"
Card 4: "Best hook: [hook style]"
Card 5: "Best platform: [top platform]"
```

This builds user confidence — they see the AI actually understands their product.

### Campaign goal

```
Awareness    → Goal: make people know this product exists
               Tone: informational, brand-building, not pushy
               CTA: "Discover now" / "Learn more" / "See the collection"

Engagement   → Goal: likes, comments, shares, saves
               Tone: relatable, fun, conversational
               CTA: "Tag someone who needs this" / "Save for later" / "Comment below"

Sales/Conversion → Goal: direct purchase clicks
               Tone: persuasive, benefit-led, urgency
               CTA: "Shop now" / "Order today" / "Get yours" / "Limited stock"

Festival/Offer → Goal: time-limited promotion
               Tone: urgent, celebratory, offer-forward
               CTA: "Diwali offer ends tonight" / "50% off — today only"
```

### Ad tone

```
Emotional    → Story-driven, connects to feelings, builds brand love
Informational → Feature-focused, educates audience, rational appeal
Humorous     → Witty, relatable, shareable, Indian humor
Urgent       → FOMO-driven, time pressure, scarcity signals
Premium      → Aspirational, luxury language, exclusivity signals
Relatable    → Everyday Indian life, slice-of-life, "this is me"
```

## PART 3: Visual Ad Creative Generation

### What the visual creative contains

Unlike Product Photography or Cinematic Ads, the Ads Creation visual has:
1. A strong background/product image (photorealistic)
2. A headline text overlay
3. A brand/product name zone
4. A CTA button area (bottom zone)
5. Proper safe zones for platform UI elements (story handles, captions etc.)

### Layout zones per format

```
INSTAGRAM FEED (1:1 or 4:5):
  Top 15%:      Logo / brand name zone
  Middle 60%:   Product + headline
  Bottom 25%:   CTA button area + safe zone

INSTAGRAM STORY / REEL COVER (9:16):
  Top 20%:      Safe zone (avoid — Instagram UI overlaps here)
  Top 20-40%:   Headline text
  Middle 40%:   Product hero
  Bottom 20%:   CTA + handle (@username area)
  Bottom 10%:   Safe zone (swipe up area)

FACEBOOK AD (1.91:1 horizontal):
  Left 50%:     Product visual
  Right 50%:    Headline + body text + CTA button area
  OR
  Full width:   Product background, headline centered overlay

YOUTUBE THUMBNAIL (16:9):
  Left 60%:     Product / emotional visual
  Right 40%:    Bold headline text (large, readable at small size)
  OR
  Full bleed:   Product + text overlay
```

### Visual creative prompt structure

The AI generates the BASE image. Text overlays, CTAs, and button graphics are added by the frontend UI layer. The prompt generates a clean compositional image with proper zones.

#### Prompt — Instagram Feed Ad Creative (1:1)
```
Professional social media advertisement image for [PRODUCT],
[COLOR] [MATERIAL] product as hero,
background: [BACKGROUND based on campaign goal + tone:
  Sales → clean white or bold brand color
  Emotional → lifestyle scene, warm tones
  Premium → dark luxury or gradient
  Festival → festive colors matching occasion],
composition: product centered slightly above middle, lower 25% clear for CTA text,
lighting: [matching tone — bright for sales/cheerful, dramatic for premium],
visual hierarchy: product is undisputed hero, everything else supports,
color palette matches [BRAND_TONE: energetic / premium / warm / fresh],
leave clean bottom zone (25% height) for text overlay,
leave top-left corner for potential logo placement,
photorealistic, commercial ad quality,
[QUALITY_SUFFIX]
```

#### Prompt — Instagram Story / Reel Cover (9:16 vertical)
```
Vertical 9:16 social media advertisement image for [PRODUCT],
[COLOR] [MATERIAL] product positioned in center-lower section of frame,
large dramatic visual impact in upper portion: [VISUAL_ELEMENT based on tone:
  Lifestyle: aspirational scene extending top-to-bottom
  Premium: dramatic dark bg with product as spotlight
  Festive: bold color field with product as center],
product occupies center 40% of frame, sharp and prominent,
top 30% area: [VISUAL_CONTENT: atmospheric sky / bold color / pattern / scene continuation],
bottom 20%: clean minimal zone for CTA (keep this area simple),
vertical composition designed for mobile-first viewing,
photorealistic, Instagram story ad quality,
[QUALITY_SUFFIX]
```

#### Prompt — Facebook Ad (1.91:1 horizontal)
```

widescreen 1.91:1 composition,

  Option A: product on left 55%, white/clean right 45% for text copy overlay

background: [BACKGROUND matching campaign goal],
color grade: [MATCHING_TONE],
designed for Facebook feed — must be eye-catching at small thumbnail size,

photorealistic, Facebook ad quality,
[QUALITY_SUFFIX]
```

#### Prompt — YouTube Thumbnail (16:9)

## PART 4: Ad Copy Generation — The 3 Versions

Every Ads Creation output generates 3 different copy versions simultaneously.
User picks the one they like, or mixes elements from different versions.

---

### VERSION 1: Emotional / Story-Driven Copy

**Philosophy:** Connect to feelings first. Product second. Make the audience feel seen.

**Structure:**
```
Line 1 (Hook):     Relatable situation or feeling — no product mention yet
Line 2-3 (Build):  Expand the feeling or story — 1-2 sentences
Line 4 (Product):  Introduce product as the solution/upgrade
Line 5 (CTA):      Warm, inviting call to action
Hashtags:          Mix of emotion + product + lifestyle tags
```

**Formula template:**
```
[RELATABLE_HOOK about target audience's life/feeling]

[1-2 sentences building on that feeling or painting a picture]

[PRODUCT_NAME] — [brief what it is] that [what it makes you feel/achieve].

[WARM_CTA]. Link in bio 🔗

[15-20 hashtags]
```

**Indian market hooks by category:**
```
Beauty/Skincare:   "Har subah ek naya confidence"
                   "Because you deserve that glow every single day"
                   "Your skin, your rules"

Home/Kitchen:      "Ghar ko thoda aur apna banana chahte ho?"
                   "The little things that make home feel like home"
                   "When your morning feels like a ritual, not a routine"

Fashion/Apparel:   "Wear what makes you feel like you"
                   "Style that speaks before you do"
                   "Outfit check: ✓"

Food/Beverage:     "Woh feeling jab pehla sip perfect hota hai"
                   "Made for those who take their [coffee/chai/food] seriously"

Electronics:       "Less time struggling. More time creating."
                   "Your new productivity partner"

Gifting:           "Kyunki kuch cheezein sirf ek SMS se express nahi hoti"
                   "The gift they didn't know they needed"
```

---

### VERSION 2: Feature / Benefit-Led Copy

**Philosophy:** Rational appeal. Clear benefits. Specific features. Why this product is worth buying.

**Structure:**
```
Line 1 (Hook):     Bold benefit statement — specific, not vague
Line 2-3 (Build):  2-3 specific features explained as benefits
Line 4 (Social proof): One credibility signal if possible
Line 5 (CTA):      Action-oriented, clear
Hashtags:          Product-specific, category, brand tags
```

**Formula template:**
```
[BOLD_BENEFIT_STATEMENT] ✨

[FEATURE 1] → [what it means for user]
[FEATURE 2] → [what it means for user]  
[FEATURE 3] → [what it means for user]

[SOCIAL_PROOF_LINE: "Loved by X customers" / "As seen on" / "Premium quality"]

[CLEAR_CTA]. Shop now → link in bio

[15-20 hashtags]
```

**Benefit translation examples:**
```
"Ceramic material"           → "Keeps your drink at perfect temperature longer"
"Dishwasher safe"            → "Zero effort cleanup — toss it in and forget it"
"350ml capacity"             → "Perfect size for your full morning coffee ritual"
"Matte finish"               → "Fingerprint-free, always looks brand new"
"BPA free"                   → "Safe for you, safe for your family"
"Handcrafted"                → "Every piece is unique — like you"
"Lightweight design"         → "Carry it everywhere without the weight"
"Premium packaging"          → "Gift-ready right out of the box"
```

---

### VERSION 3: Urgency / Offer-Led Copy

**Philosophy:** Create FOMO. Time pressure. Scarcity. Irresistible offer framing.

**Structure:**
```
Line 1 (Urgency hook): Time-limited or scarcity signal
Line 2 (Offer):        The deal, clearly stated
Line 3 (Value):        Why this is worth acting on NOW
Line 4 (CTA):          Immediate action command
Line 5 (Timer):        "Offer ends [date/time]" if applicable
Hashtags:              Sale/offer tags + product tags
```

**Formula template:**
```
[URGENCY_SIGNAL] ⚡

[OFFER_STATEMENT — clear, specific, no ambiguity]

[PRODUCT] — [brief description] at [price or % off].
[VALUE_JUSTIFICATION: "Worth every rupee" / "Our bestseller" / "Only X left"]

[STRONG_CTA]: Link in bio 👆

Offer valid till [DATE/TIME] only.

[hashtags]
```

**Urgency triggers for Indian market:**
```
Festival:        "Diwali gift sorted! ✨ Special Diwali price — today only"
Scarcity:        "Only 47 pieces left in stock. Once gone, gone."
Flash sale:      "⚡ Flash sale — next 4 hours only"
Weekend:         "Weekend special — extra 10% off, only till Sunday midnight"
New launch:      "Just launched! First 100 orders get free shipping"
Monsoon:         "Monsoon refresh: everything under ₹999 this week"
Pay day:         "Pay day sale 🎉 — your cart has been waiting for you"
Year end:        "Last chance — year-end clearance, up to 50% off"
```

## PART 5: Hashtag Generation System

### 3-tier hashtag strategy

Every output gets 15-20 hashtags organized in this structure:

**Tier 1 — Broad reach (5-6 tags):** High volume, general discovery
**Tier 2 — Mid-range (6-8 tags):** Category + niche specific, qualified audience
**Tier 3 — Branded/Long-tail (3-4 tags):** Very specific, high intent, low competition

## PART 8: A/B Testing Feature

### What A/B testing generates

When user clicks "Generate A/B Variant":

```
VARIANT A (already shown):  Original creative + Copy version chosen by user
VARIANT B (new):            Different creative angle + Different copy hook

B variant changes:
  - Different background/composition for the visual
  - Different copy hook (if A was emotional, B is benefit-led)
  - Different CTA phrasing
  - Different first line (headline)

Show both side-by-side with labels "A" and "B"
User can download both and test which performs better
```

### A/B guidance text shown to user

> "Version A aur B ko alag-alag post karo aur dekho kaunsa zyada engage karta hai. Generally: emotional copy women-focused products pe better karta hai, benefit-led copy electronics pe."

## PART 9: Animation States

### Full animation sequence

```
STEP 1 — Upload received:
  → Quick product scan animation (same as other tools, 1.5s)
  
STEP 2 — Marketing brief building (unique to this tool):
  → "Marketing brief ban raha hai..." text
  → 5 brief cards animate in one by one (product, audience, USP, hook, platform)
  → Each card has a small icon: product tag, person, star, hook, platform logo
  → Duration: ~3 seconds total for all 5 cards

STEP 3 — Campaign generation (3 parallel streams):
  → 3 progress bars appear simultaneously
  → Bar 1: "Ad creative" — left to right fill
  → Bar 2: "Copy writing" — left to right fill (slightly offset timing)
  → Bar 3: "Hashtags + extras" — left to right fill (slightly offset)
  → Text cycling below: "Analyzing competitors..." → "Writing 3 copy versions..." → 
    "Designing your creative..." → "Almost ready..."
  → Duration: loops until generation complete

STEP 4 — Output reveal:
  → Ad creative image appears with TYPEWRITER ANIMATION on headline text
    (text types itself onto the image from left to right, like a real ad being made)
  → Below image: 3 copy version cards SLIDE IN from right, one by one (0.3s each)
  → Hashtag cloud FADES IN below copy cards
  → Duration: ~2 seconds for full reveal sequence

STEP 5 — Actions appear:
  → Approve / Regenerate / A/B Test buttons fade in
  → Platform export selector appears below
```

## PART 11: Quality Standards

### Visual creative checklist

```
□ Product clearly identifiable in final creative
□ Text overlay zones are clean and readable (if frontend adds text here)
□ Color palette matches the chosen campaign tone
□ Creative would stop a scroll (not generic, not boring)
□ Platform aspect ratio is correct
□ Bottom/top safe zones respected for story formats
□ Creative doesn't look like a generic Canva template
□ Brand-quality feel — not amateur
```

### Copy quality checklist

```
□ Hook is attention-grabbing (would make target audience pause)
□ Each of the 3 versions has a distinctly different angle
□ No grammatical errors in either Hindi, English, or Hinglish
□ CTA is clear and specific
□ Hashtags are relevant — no random unrelated tags
□ Copy length appropriate for platform (Twitter ≠ Instagram)
□ Emotional copy actually has emotion (not just "great product!")
□ Urgency copy has actual urgency (not vague "buy now")
```

### Benchmark targets

```
Visual:     Mamaearth, boAt, Sugar Cosmetics, Boat, Lenskart, Zomato ad quality
Copy:       Swiggy India, Nykaa, Blinkit social media copywriting standard
Hashtags:   Relevant, not spammy — like a real social media manager would choose
Overall:    Could this run as a paid Facebook/Instagram ad? If yes, it passes.
```

## PART 13: System Prompt for Ads Creation AI

```
You are Pixalera's Ads Creation AI. You think like an experienced Indian performance 
marketing expert and social media manager. Your job is to take a product image and 
turn it into a complete, ready-to-post marketing campaign.

YOUR OUTPUT ALWAYS CONTAINS:
1. A platform-optimized ad creative image (photorealistic, scroll-stopping)
2. 3 different ad copy versions (emotional, benefit-led, urgency)
3. 15-20 platform-relevant hashtags
4. Platform-specific extras (story text, YouTube titles, WhatsApp message, etc.)

MARKETING INTELLIGENCE FIRST:
Before generating anything, deeply analyze:
- Who is the target audience (be specific: not "women" but "women 25-35 who care about aesthetics")
- What is the ONE core USP of this product
- What pain point does it solve OR what desire does it trigger
- What is the best hook style for this product and audience
- What season/festival is relevant right now

COPY QUALITY STANDARD:
```
